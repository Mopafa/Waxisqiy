**KR Puram Local Bulletin: Saturday, April 25, 2026**

### **1. Traffic Update: Tin Factory & Hanging Bridge**
*   **Current Status:** Heavy congestion is reported at the **Tin Factory junction** and the **KR Puram Hanging Bridge**, particularly for commuters moving toward Hebbal and Mahadevapura.
*   **Hebbal Flyover Alert:** The prohibition of vehicle movement on the **KR Puram up-ramp** of the Hebbal flyover remains in effect due to ongoing BDA infrastructure work (adding additional lanes).
*   **Recommended Routes:** Commuters heading toward the city center are advised to use **Lingarajapuram Flyover** or **Nagavara-Tannery Road** to avoid the bottlenecks at the Hebbal intersection.
*   **Reminder:** Note that the bus stop previously at the mouth of the Hanging Bridge has been permanently shifted 100 meters back toward the skywalk to help streamline traffic flow.

### **2. Civic Alerts & Local News**
*   **Power Supply:** **No scheduled power cuts** are planned by BESCOM for any Bengaluru zones today. However, local fluctuations may occur due to infrastructure maintenance.
*   **BBMP/GBA Activity:** A **demolition drive** was conducted in the KR Puram area today by the newly formed Bengaluru East Corporation (formerly BBMP). Local reports indicate a protest incident where a couple was rescued after a self-immolation threat against the drive. Residents in areas with unauthorized structures should stay alert to continued enforcement.
*   **Governance Change:** Residents are reminded that KR Puram now falls under the **Bengaluru East Corporation** following the implementation of the Greater Bengaluru Governance Act.

### **3. Holi 2026 & Weekend Events**
*   **Holi Status:** **Holi 2026 concluded on March 4, 2026.** There are no official Holi celebrations scheduled for today, April 25.
*   **Upcoming Festivals:** Residents can look forward to **Narasimha Jayanti** celebrations tomorrow, Sunday (April 26), and **Akshaya Tritiya** on April 29.
*   **Local Event Today:** The **MTR Karunadu Swada Food Festival** is happening today and tomorrow at Jayamahal Palace, featuring over 100 traditional Karnataka dishes and folk performances.

**Safety Tip:** With temperatures rising this weekend, residents are advised to stay hydrated and avoid non-essential travel during peak afternoon hours (12 PM – 4 PM).
