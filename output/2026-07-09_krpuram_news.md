**Local News Bulletin: KR Puram, Bengaluru**  
**Date:** July 9, 2026  
**Status:** Live Updates for Residents

---

### **1. Traffic Update: Tin Factory & Hanging Bridge**
*   **Current Status:** Expect **heavy slow-moving traffic** at the Tin Factory junction and the KR Puram Hanging Bridge (Cable-Stayed Bridge) during peak evening hours (5:00 PM – 8:30 PM).
*   **Key Choke Points:** The "bottleneck" under the KR Puram bridge remains the primary area for gridlocks. Ongoing pressure from outstation buses stopping at the mouth of the bridge continues to disrupt the flow towards Whitefield and Hebbal.
*   **Advisory:** Commuters are advised to use the **Purple Line Metro** to bypass the Tin Factory-KR Puram stretch. If driving, consider the **Kasturinagar-Banaswadi** internal routes to avoid the main ORR (Outer Ring Road) flyover congestion.

### **2. Local Civic Alerts (BBMP/BTP)**
*   **Mandatory E-Khata:** As of July 1, 2026, the BBMP has made **E-Khata mandatory** for all building plan approvals and property transactions. Residents in KR Puram are urged to digitize their property records via the BBMP portal or visit the local Ward Office to avoid delays in property-related services.
*   **Monsoon Warning:** With the monsoon in full swing, residents are cautioned against **waterlogging** in low-lying areas near the KR Puram Lake and the Lowry Under Bridge area. BBMP emergency teams (Helpline: 1533) are on standby for any fallen trees or drainage overflows.
*   **Safety Notice:** Following a recent high-speed accident on the Tin Factory flyover, traffic police have increased surveillance and patrolling to curb underage driving and speeding during late-night hours.

### **3. Festival Clarification: Holi 2026**
*   **Correction:** Contrary to some local queries, **Holi 2026 is NOT today**. Holi (the Festival of Colors) was celebrated on **March 4, 2026**.
*   **Upcoming Festivals:** Residents should instead prepare for the **Jagannath Rath Yatra**, which is scheduled for **July 16, 2026**. Major local processions and celebrations are expected at ISKCON Whitefield and Bhartiya City (Hennur-Bellahalli area) later this month.
