### **KR Puram Local News Bulletin: March 28, 2026**

**Current Date:** Saturday, March 28, 2026  
**Status:** Post-Holi Season | IPL Match Day

---

#### **1. Traffic & Transit: Tin Factory / Hanging Bridge**
*   **Major Bottleneck Alert:** Expect high congestion at the **Tin Factory junction** and the **KR Puram Hanging Bridge** due to ongoing road widening and metro-related infrastructure works.
*   **IPL Impact:** The **RCB vs SRH** match at M. Chinnaswamy Stadium today is causing major traffic restrictions in the Central Business District (CBD). Residents traveling toward the city center from KR Puram via Old Madras Road should expect heavy ripple-effect traffic starting from 4:00 PM. 
*   **Local Diversion:** The **Lowry Underpass** remains restricted for heavy vehicles due to ongoing railway bridge work; two-wheelers are permitted, but four-wheelers are advised to use the Pai Layout U-turn.

#### **2. Local Civic Alerts (Bengaluru East City Corporation)**
*   **Maiden Budget Highlights:** Following the dissolution of the BBMP, the newly formed **Bengaluru East City Corporation (BECC)** presented its first budget (₹3,890 crore) yesterday. Key projects approved for the KR Puram/East zone include:
    *   **ORR Reconstruction:** A ₹450 crore project to strengthen the Outer Ring Road from Silk Board to KR Puram.
    *   **New Grade Separator:** Allocated ₹50 crore for the Alpine Eco Road junction on ORR to ease bottlenecks.
    *   **Pedestrian Safety:** Plans to construct 100 km of new footpaths in the East zone this fiscal year.
*   **Khata Conversion:** The BECC has intensified the digitization of property documents. Residents with 'B khata' properties are encouraged to check the official GBA (Greater Bengaluru Authority) portal for the newly announced 'A khata' conversion scheme.

#### **3. Holi 2026 Celebrations**
*   **Festival Status:** The main Holi festival (Rangwali Holi) was celebrated on **March 4, 2026**. There are **no major public Holi events or celebrations** scheduled for today as the festival season concluded earlier this month.
*   **Weekend Vibes:** While no color-throwing events are active, local venues in nearby Indiranagar and Whitefield may host standard Saturday night themed events, but the "Festival of Colors" is officially over.

#### **4. Utility & Safety Watch**
*   **Water Conservation:** The city-wide ban on using drinking water for non-essential purposes (car washing, gardening, etc.) remains in force. The BWSSB is actively patrolling and imposing **₹5,000 fines** for violations.
*   **Power Supply:** No major scheduled power shutdowns are reported for the KR Puram zone today; however, routine maintenance in the Electronic City station may cause minor fluctuations in parts of Mahadevapura.
