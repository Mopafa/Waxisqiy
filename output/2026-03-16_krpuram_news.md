### **KR Puram Local News Bulletin: Monday, March 16, 2026**

#### **1. Traffic Update: Tin Factory & Hanging Bridge**
*   **Current Status:** Traffic remains **heavy and slow-moving** at the Tin Factory junction and the K.R. Puram Hanging Bridge.
*   **Delays:** Expect a minimum delay of **15–20 minutes** during peak hours. The congestion is primarily driven by ongoing **Phase IIB Metro construction** (K.R. Puram to Bengaluru Airport line) and the convergence of freight vehicles entering from Old Madras Road.
*   **Advisory:** The automated signals at the mouth of the bridge are operational, but "free-for-all" conditions are reported at the Kasturinagar ramp. Commuters heading toward Marathahalli/Silk Board are advised to use service lanes where possible, though bus-bay overcrowding near the bridge remains a bottleneck.

#### **2. Local Civic Alerts**
*   **Power Supply:** **BESCOM** has confirmed there are **no scheduled power cuts** for KR Puram or the wider Bengaluru East zone today.
*   **LPG Crisis:** A severe shortage of commercial **LPG cylinders** is affecting local eateries and PG accommodations in the KR Puram and Mahadevapura areas. Some restaurants have reduced their menus or introduced a "gas surcharge."
*   **Infrastructure News:** Following the recent state budget (March 6), the **East-West Corridor project** (linking K.R. Puram to Mysore Road via a tunnel/elevated road) has been officially approved. Tenders for the first phase are expected shortly.
*   **Tax Dues:** The Greater Bengaluru Authority (GBA) has recently initiated auctions for properties in KR Puram with significant pending property tax dues; residents are advised to clear any outstanding balances via the GBA portal.

#### **3. Holi 2026 & Local Events**
*   **Holi Update:** The official Holi (Rangwali Holi) celebrations for 2026 took place on **March 4**. While the main public festivities have concluded, a few club-based "Phoolon ki Holi" (Flower Holi) events are reportedly continuing in central Bengaluru until March 25.
*   **Happening Today:** 
    *   **Whitefield Art Collective (VR Bengaluru):** The 9th edition of this month-long public art festival is currently live at VR Bengaluru (near KR Puram). Residents can visit to see over 300 art installations and the "Kala Car" (a Tesla transformed into a moving canvas).
    *   **Cult Fitness Carnival:** A large-scale fitness event is scheduled at **KTPO, Whitefield** today, featuring celebrity-led workshops.
    *   **Science Festival:** Preparation is underway at Bangalore University for the "Women in Science" festival starting tomorrow (March 17).

**Resident Tip:** Due to the heat intensifying and the LPG crisis, residents are encouraged to use the **Sahaaya 2.0 app** to report local water or waste management issues directly to the ward office.
