**LOCAL BULLETIN: KR PURAM, BANGALORE**
**Date:** Wednesday, March 18, 2026

### **1. Traffic Update: Tin Factory & Hanging Bridge**
*   **Congestion Alert:** Expect heavy gridlock at the **Tin Factory junction** and the **KR Puram Hanging Bridge** throughout the day. Traffic is significantly impacted by the ongoing **Outer Ring Road (ORR) Facelift Project**, which began earlier this year. Partial road closures between Silk Board and KR Puram for this 11-month infrastructure overhaul are causing bottlenecks.
*   **Peak Hours:** Congestion is expected to peak between **8:00 AM – 10:30 AM** and **5:00 PM – 8:30 PM**. Commuters traveling toward Marathahalli or Hebbal are advised to use the ITPL road or alternate inner routes to avoid the bridge bottleneck.
*   **Metro Impact:** Ongoing work on the foot-over-bridge at the KR Puram Metro station continues to narrow lanes on the ORR, adding to the slow movement of vehicles.

### **2. Local Civic Alerts**
*   **SSLC Exams Begin Today:** The Karnataka SSLC (Class 10) Board Examinations commence today, March 18. Residents should expect a higher volume of students and parents near schools and exam centers in the KR Puram area during the morning hours (**10:00 AM – 1:15 PM**).
*   **Free BMTC Travel:** To support students, the **BMTC is providing free bus travel** today. Students can board any regular (non-AC) BMTC bus for free by showing their 2026 SSLC Hall Ticket.
*   **Power & Utilities:** No scheduled power cuts have been reported by BESCOM for the KR Puram division today.
*   **Weather Warning:** The IMD has predicted a **90–100% chance of thunderstorms** and pre-monsoon showers in Bangalore within the next 48 hours. Residents are advised to clear drains and secure outdoor items.

### **3. Holi 2026 & Upcoming Festivities**
*   **Holi Status:** While the primary Holi celebrations in Bangalore took place on **March 4, 2026**, a few late-season themed events remain active. The "Phoolon Ki Holi" (Flower Holi) series at **Nolimmits Lounge** is ongoing today for those still looking to celebrate.
*   **Ugadi Eve:** Today marks the eve of **Ugadi (New Year)**, which falls tomorrow, March 19. Expect increased activity and crowds at local markets near the KR Puram railway station as residents shop for festive essentials like neem, jaggery, and mango leaves.
*   **Note:** Due to the start of board exams, many residential associations have restricted loud music and public color-play for today to maintain a quiet environment for students.
